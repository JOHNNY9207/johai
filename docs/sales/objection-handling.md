# Objection Handling

Address accuracy with approved knowledge and human approval; address security with the current tenant roadmap; address ROI with measured pilots rather than unsupported claims; disclose partial billing and integration features.

## Status discipline

See [Product Fact Sheet](../PRODUCT_FACT_SHEET.md) for Implemented, Partial, and Planned classifications. Update this chapter when the corresponding product behavior changes.

## Constitutional objection handling

- **“Is this another chatbot?”** No. The product direction is an intelligent business operating system that uses conversation only when it adds value. Current chat capability and Planned contextual experiences must still be described separately.
- **“Will AI replace my team?”** No such promise is allowed. JOHAI supports accountable work, preserves human authority, and requires natural human takeover where judgment or policy demands it.
- **“Can it run everything automatically?”** Do not promise unrestricted autonomy. Explain which actions are implemented, which require confirmation, which remain Planned, and where JOHAI must stop.
- **“How can I trust it?”** Point to permission boundaries, evidence labels, uncertainty disclosure, failure behavior, human approval, and measured validation—not generic AI confidence.
- **“Why JOHAI?”** The represented business remains the hero. JOHAI should reduce effort, create trust, remove friction, and help the business deliver a better experience.

See the [JOHAI Constitution](../constitution/JOHAI_CONSTITUTION.md) and [Language Guidelines](../constitution/LANGUAGE_GUIDELINES.md). Do not use Constitutional principles to overstate current product capability.

## Foundation-backed responses

- **“What does JOHAI stand for?”** Use the [North Star](../foundation/JOHAI_NORTH_STAR.md), [Mission and Vision](../foundation/MISSION_AND_VISION.md), and [Manifesto](../foundation/JOHAI_MANIFESTO.md): JOHAI exists to make serious, trustworthy operating intelligence practical for owner-led businesses while relationships and human authority remain central.
- **“What can customers expect?”** Use the [Product Promise](../foundation/PRODUCT_PROMISE.md), then separate permanent experience commitments from the current Implemented, Partial, and Planned capability record.
- **“How will AI Employees behave?”** Use the [AI Employee Principles](../foundation/AI_EMPLOYEE_PRINCIPLES.md): bounded roles, explicit limits, permission discipline, honest uncertainty, natural escalation, and no fake expertise. Do not imply that every named role or autonomous runtime is implemented.
- **“Is this just brand language?”** Explain that the [Company Values](../foundation/COMPANY_VALUES.md), [Culture](../foundation/CULTURE.md), [Brand Guidelines](../foundation/BRAND_GUIDELINES.md), and [Voice and Tone](../foundation/VOICE_AND_TONE.md) are adopted decision standards. They do not replace product evidence, security review, customer validation, or delivery proof.
