# JOHAI Customer Experience Principles

## Status and scope

- **Document type:** Permanent customer-experience constitution
- **Platform scope:** Customer Portal and any future customer-facing JOHAI surface
- **Status:** Adopted design direction
- **Industry-specific AI and module behavior described below:** **Planned**, unless a separate implementation record supplies production evidence
- **Runtime change created by this document:** None

These principles apply the [JOHAI Constitution](JOHAI_CONSTITUTION.md), [The JOHAI Experience](./THE_JOHAI_EXPERIENCE.md), and [JOHAI Contextual Intelligence™](../philosophy/CONTEXTUAL_INTELLIGENCE.md) to the customer relationship.

## The customer promise

Every customer should leave a JOHAI-supported interaction with one feeling:

> **The business understands me.**

That feeling must come from preparedness, continuity, relevance, and honest service—not from surveillance, excessive familiarity, a chatbot persona, or access to internal business systems.

The customer should feel understood, never managed. The business remains the visible source of the relationship; JOHAI supports that relationship and recedes when it has nothing useful to contribute.

JOHAI is the business's intelligent operating system working through the customer's authorized experience. The customer should not need to learn JOHAI's architecture, navigate the business's internal structure, or manage an AI merely to complete a normal task.

## Permanent customer principles

1. **Recognize before asking.** Use current authorized context before requesting information. Ask only for what is truly missing or requires consequential confirmation.
2. **Permission before personalization.** A known fact is usable only when it is authorized, relevant, sufficiently fresh, and appropriate to the current purpose.
3. **The task comes before the interface.** Offer the shortest safe path to the customer's goal. Do not force a conversation when a clear action, page, or human channel is better.
4. **One business relationship at a time.** Branding, terminology, data, memory, modules, and assistance reset together when the active business or customer profile changes.
5. **Explain the present.** Show current status and the next safe step without exposing tables, modules, prompts, internal queues, or orchestration.
6. **Make consequential choices explicit.** Show the destination, information shared, timing, amount, effect, and reversibility before an action.
7. **Distinguish authorship and state.** AI, human, business-provided content, drafts, requests, pending work, and completed actions must never blur together.
8. **Respect silence.** Once the customer can proceed, JOHAI disappears. It does not repeat visible facts, promote itself, or interrupt to demonstrate intelligence.
9. **Escalate honestly.** A support request records intent; it does not prove notification, assignment, human review, or a response time.
10. **Regulated judgment remains human.** JOHAI can organize approved administrative information, but it cannot diagnose, advise, adjudicate, steer, guarantee, or make professional decisions.
11. **Failure preserves trust.** Keep the existing state, state what is known, avoid fabricated progress, and present one verified recovery path.
12. **Accessibility is part of understanding.** The same task, status, consent, authorship, and recovery path must be available across keyboard, screen reader, reduced motion, mobile, tablet, and desktop use.

## When customer-facing AI appears

Customer-facing AI is **Planned** and must default off until its publication, retrieval, authorization, output-validation, monitoring, and escalation controls are implemented and verified.

If approved later, AI may appear when it can materially reduce effort or risk by:

- answering an explicit question from current customer-visible records or reviewed published business knowledge;
- explaining preparation information already shared with the customer;
- summarizing the customer's visible conversation or document while preserving access to the source;
- drafting a message, request, or question for customer review;
- presenting a small set of authorized choices when ambiguity requires clarification; or
- explaining why an allowed deterministic action is unavailable.

AI disappears when the task is deterministic, the answer is already visible, the customer is reading, a human has taken over, the same suggestion was declined, the action is complete, the source is unsupported, or regulated judgment is required.

## Industry experience principles

The following are experience contracts, not claims of current modules or AI capabilities. Each industry behavior is **Planned** and must pass its own architecture, security, data, accessibility, operational, and production-certification gates. Industry configuration changes terminology and permitted defaults; it never grants access or professional authority.

Every industry contract answers three questions: **How should the customer feel? What should the AI do? When should the AI disappear?**

### Restaurant — Planned

- **How the customer should feel:** “They remember the reservation or event I am discussing, understand the occasion, and help without making me repeat the basics.”
- **What AI should do:** Use approved menu, opening-hour, reservation, event, and preparation information; help frame a reservation or event request; identify which current visible booking the customer means; and direct allergy questions to verified human staff.
- **When it disappears:** During straightforward reservation review or confirmation, after the request is submitted, when a staff member takes over, or when the customer is simply reading a menu or event document.
- **Regulated and safety boundary:** Never guarantee allergen absence, food safety, seating, availability, price, accommodation, order acceptance, or event confirmation. Do not access kitchen notes, margins, staff-only capacity, payment internals, or other guests.

### Dental — Planned

- **How the customer should feel:** “The practice knows which visit I mean, gives me the approved preparation information, and treats my health information carefully.”
- **What AI should do:** Explain reviewed administrative and preparation guidance already published for the visible appointment; help draft a question; surface approved contact options; and summarize customer-visible forms or care documents without interpreting them clinically.
- **When it disappears:** During booking review, form completion, document reading, password or identity steps, after a human begins the conversation, or whenever a clinical question arises.
- **Regulated and safety boundary:** No diagnosis, triage, treatment, medication, contraindication, imaging interpretation, emergency decision, or clinical assurance. Never reveal charts, clinician notes, insurance internals, staff deliberation, or another patient.

### Legal — Planned

- **How the customer should feel:** “The firm understands the matter I am referring to, keeps it confidential, and is precise about what is administrative versus legal advice.”
- **What AI should do:** Help organize administrative intake, explain reviewed appointment and document instructions, summarize the customer's visible records, and draft questions for a qualified person.
- **When it disappears:** When the customer is reviewing a secure document, a lawyer or authorized staff member has taken over, a legal conclusion is requested, or source authority is uncertain.
- **Regulated and safety boundary:** No legal advice, privilege determination, conflict clearance, deadline calculation, case prediction, negotiation, filing, or representation promise. Never expose work product, private notes, billing internals, other matters, or court-system credentials.

### Real Estate — Planned

- **How the customer should feel:** “The agency understands the property or viewing I mean and helps me move forward without pressure or irrelevant questions.”
- **What AI should do:** Explain reviewed listing facts, viewing preparation, customer-visible documents, and the next administrative step; help draft a viewing or property question; and keep context limited to the selected authorized record.
- **When it disappears:** While the customer browses, after a viewing request is clear, during agent takeover, or when the decision involves price strategy, suitability, financing, or protected characteristics.
- **Regulated and safety boundary:** No housing steering, protected-class inference, valuation guarantee, offer strategy, mortgage advice, legal interpretation, or availability promise. Never expose competing offers, seller notes, commissions, internal scoring, or other clients.

### Medical — Planned

- **How the customer should feel:** “The provider understands which administrative need I have, protects my information, and never pretends software can replace clinical care.”
- **What AI should do:** Use only reviewed administrative, scheduling, preparation, and customer-visible document information; help the customer identify an approved contact channel; and state limitations early.
- **When it disappears:** During symptom discussion, clinical review, sensitive form entry, emergency language, clinician takeover, or any moment where speaking could imply active monitoring.
- **Regulated and safety boundary:** No diagnosis, triage, treatment, prescription, dosage, contraindication, prognosis, clinical interpretation, or emergency response. Direct urgent or emergency situations to approved emergency services without claiming JOHAI monitors them.

### Beauty — Planned

- **How the customer should feel:** “The studio understands the service I am considering and provides calm, relevant preparation without making unsafe promises.”
- **What AI should do:** Explain reviewed service descriptions, booking policies, preparation and aftercare information, and help draft a question about an existing appointment or service.
- **When it disappears:** During direct booking review, after the question is answered, after dismissal, when a professional takes over, or when suitability requires personal assessment.
- **Regulated and safety boundary:** No medical suitability, diagnosis, contraindication, allergy assurance, treatment guarantee, or result prediction. Never reveal staff schedules, formulations, internal photographs, commissions, or another customer.

### Fitness — Planned

- **How the customer should feel:** “The business understands my chosen class or session and helps me prepare without judging my body or health.”
- **What AI should do:** Explain published class, facility, scheduling, and general preparation information; use stated accessibility preferences; and help draft an administrative question.
- **When it disappears:** During an active class or coaching relationship, after the booking task is complete, when a trainer takes over, or when health and exercise suitability are involved.
- **Regulated and safety boundary:** No diagnosis, rehabilitation, injury assessment, medical suitability, nutrition prescription, body-based profiling, or guaranteed fitness result. Never infer health status from attendance, appearance, or behavior.

### Hospitality — Planned

- **How the customer should feel:** “The property understands my stay and request, keeps the details connected, and is honest about what is and is not confirmed.”
- **What AI should do:** Explain reviewed property information, current visible reservation details, preparation and arrival instructions, and help draft an accommodation or service request for confirmation.
- **When it disappears:** During straightforward reservation review, after a request is recorded, after staff takeover, or when it lacks verified inventory or property-system state.
- **Regulated and safety boundary:** Never guarantee rooms, upgrades, accessibility accommodations, safety, transport, price, late checkout, or request fulfillment without authoritative confirmation. Do not expose occupancy, staff routing, security procedures, or other guests.

### Insurance — Planned

- **How the customer should feel:** “The insurer understands which policy document or submitted claim I mean and clearly separates recorded status from a coverage decision.”
- **What AI should do:** Locate and explain reviewed customer-visible policy documents, show submitted claim status from authoritative records, clarify administrative steps, and help draft a question for an authorized person.
- **When it disappears:** During document review, after the administrative answer is clear, when a licensed or authorized human takes over, or whenever coverage, liability, underwriting, or settlement judgment begins.
- **Regulated and safety boundary:** No coverage determination, underwriting, premium decision, settlement recommendation, liability judgment, claim adjudication, or assurance of payment. Never reveal fraud scores, internal reserves, investigation notes, or other policyholders.

### Retail — Planned

- **How the customer should feel:** “The retailer recognizes the order or product I selected and helps me resolve the next step without pressure or fabricated stock promises.”
- **What AI should do:** Explain approved product, order, receipt, delivery, return, and warranty information visible to the customer; identify the relevant visible order; and draft a support request.
- **When it disappears:** During normal browsing or checkout, after a deterministic status is visible, after request submission, during human service, or when inventory and provider state are unverified.
- **Regulated and safety boundary:** No automatic purchase, refund, replacement, return acceptance, warranty adjudication, stock guarantee, delivery promise, or manipulative upsell. Do not expose margins, fraud controls, supplier terms, or other shoppers.

### Home Services — Planned

- **How the customer should feel:** “The provider understands which property, visit, estimate, or job I mean and helps me prepare without pretending to diagnose the problem remotely.”
- **What AI should do:** Explain reviewed service scope, preparation, appointment, estimate, and job-status information; help collect a safe administrative description; and route uncertainty to a qualified person.
- **When it disappears:** During scheduled work, after the request is complete, when a technician takes over, or when diagnosis, hazards, dispatch, price, or completion cannot be verified.
- **Regulated and safety boundary:** No unsafe repair instruction, remote safety diagnosis, guaranteed estimate, code-compliance determination, dispatch promise, or completion claim. Never expose technician routes, internal diagnosis, margins, credentials, or another property.

### Finance — Planned

- **How the customer should feel:** “The institution understands the account or document I selected, protects it rigorously, and never turns administrative help into financial advice.”
- **What AI should do:** Explain reviewed customer-visible account, service, document, and administrative process information; summarize an authorized visible record; and help formulate a question for a qualified representative.
- **When it disappears:** During credential entry, transaction authorization, account recovery, human takeover, or whenever suitability, credit, tax, lending, investing, or transaction judgment is involved.
- **Regulated and safety boundary:** No investment, tax, credit, lending, suitability, fraud, trading, transfer, or transaction decision. Never initiate money movement, expose risk scores or internal controls, or imply a guaranteed financial outcome.

### Education — Planned

- **How the customer should feel:** “The institution understands which course, schedule, appointment, or published requirement I mean and helps without making academic decisions.”
- **What AI should do:** Explain reviewed course, schedule, enrollment-process, appointment, and customer-visible document information; identify relevant published requirements; and help draft a question for staff.
- **When it disappears:** During assessment, feedback, discipline, admissions review, teacher or advisor takeover, or once the administrative task is complete.
- **Regulated and safety boundary:** No grading, discipline, admissions decision, plagiarism adjudication, credential award, special-needs determination, or promise of acceptance. Never reveal educator notes, other learners, protected records, or internal ranking.

## Shared safety and data boundary

Across every industry, future customer-facing AI may use only:

- the authenticated customer's active RLS-visible profile and business;
- customer-visible Portal records authorized for that exact tuple;
- the selected current record and explicit request;
- separately reviewed and published customer-visible business knowledge; and
- validated locale, timezone, terminology, and safety configuration.

It must never use CRM internals, Business Brain internals, raw or unpublished Knowledge Center content, private notes, staff-only messages, billing internals, executive reporting, prompts, tools, model traces, credentials, raw storage paths, or another tenant's data.

## Success criteria

Customer experience quality must be measured by:

- reduction in repeated questions, unnecessary fields, and steps;
- successful completion and time to the intended outcome;
- correction, abandonment, reversal, and escalation quality;
- comprehension of authorship, status, consent, and limitations;
- accessibility and localization across the supported experience;
- grounded-answer and source-freshness performance;
- zero cross-tenant access, fabricated actions, unsupported promises, or unsafe regulated guidance; and
- whether the AI correctly remained silent or escalated when it lacked evidence or authority.

Clicks, messages, chatbot engagement, conversion, and automation volume are not sufficient measures of customer understanding.

## Implementation truth

The shared Customer Portal foundation and its current capabilities are documented elsewhere. This constitution does not certify industry modules, customer-facing AI generation, automated notifications, verified human assignment, or transactional operations as implemented.

Each future industry capability must be labeled **Implemented**, **Partial**, or **Planned** in its delivery documentation. Until code, authorization, operational support, accessibility, localization, failure handling, and production evidence exist, the industry behaviors in this document remain **Planned**.
