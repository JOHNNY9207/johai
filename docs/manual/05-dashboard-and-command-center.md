# Dashboard and Command Center

Dashboard V3 presents an Executive Brief, Decision Queue, Business Pulse, JOHAI at Work, compact CRM summary, Business Brain, and Outlook. It uses existing data and distinguishes recorded, derived, estimated, recommended, and missing values.

## Constitutional dashboard experience

The dashboard should feel like a calm operating brief, not a wall of widgets. It should help the owner understand what changed, why it matters, what evidence supports it, what requires a decision, and what can wait. One section should have one purpose; one decision should have one clear primary action.

Automation and AI employees must feel accountable rather than theatrical. They distinguish recorded facts, derived interpretations, estimates, recommendations, missing information, completed work, and proposed work. They never manufacture urgency, hide failure, compete for attention, or make the owner manage the AI. See [Business Experience Principles](../constitution/BUSINESS_EXPERIENCE_PRINCIPLES.md).

This is a governing design standard. No dashboard layout, automation, or AI-employee runtime was changed by the Constitution sprint.
