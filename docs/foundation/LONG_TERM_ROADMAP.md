# JOHAI Long-Term Roadmap

## Authority and status

- **Document type:** Directional strategic roadmap
- **Status:** Adopted direction; all unverified items remain `Planned` or deliberately unavailable
- **Authority:** Subordinate to applicable law, binding obligations, security, privacy, accessibility, explicit human approval, mandatory database safety, and the [JOHAI Constitution](../constitution/JOHAI_CONSTITUTION.md)
- **Runtime effect:** None
- **Delivery effect:** None; a horizon is not a date commitment, funded plan, market promise, or implementation authorization

This roadmap describes how JOHAI may evolve over one, three, five, and ten years while remaining faithful to the [North Star](JOHAI_NORTH_STAR.md), [Mission and Vision](MISSION_AND_VISION.md), [Product Promise](PRODUCT_PROMISE.md), [AI Employee Principles](AI_EMPLOYEE_PRINCIPLES.md), and [Company Values](COMPANY_VALUES.md).

Every public and internal statement must retain the current evidence-based capability status in the [Product Fact Sheet](../PRODUCT_FACT_SHEET.md). Items may move, narrow, remain Planned, or become deliberately unavailable as evidence, law, risk, resources, and customer needs change.

## Strategic sequence

- **One year — dependable foundations:** make the current product coherent, secure, accessible, observable, and evidence-backed before expanding scope.
- **Three years — governed connection:** connect proven capabilities through stable contracts and carefully expand participation.
- **Five years — extensible contextual platform:** support a mature modular ecosystem without weakening identity, permission, or human authority.
- **Ten years — trusted international operating layer:** pursue durable infrastructure for responsible business-customer coordination across markets.

The sequence is more important than the dates. Foundation risk cannot be skipped to reach an ecosystem or international ambition.

## One-year horizon — dependable foundations

### Platform

- Clarify ownership and contracts across the Public Website, Business Workspace, Customer Portal, AI Layer, JOHAI Super Admin, and Developer Platform.
- Strengthen identity, tenant isolation, permission, audit, accessibility, localization, status, failure, recovery, monitoring, and documentation for implemented workflows.
- Establish shared semantic state, design-token, component, and language foundations without merging platform-layer authorization.
- Finish production evidence for bounded capabilities before describing them as ready.

### AI

- Define and approve the first bounded AI Employee role contracts.
- Prioritize retrieval, preparation, summarization, translation, drafting, classification, and reviewable recommendations before external action.
- Establish provenance, uncertainty, evaluation, abstention, approval, audit, stop, and verified human-handoff patterns.
- Keep regulated judgment and unsupported autonomous action deliberately unavailable.

### Marketplace

- Define marketplace participation, review, permission disclosure, pricing truth, provider accountability, incident, suspension, removal, and customer-remedy rules.
- Research a limited catalog of reviewed integrations, templates, or role extensions.
- Keep a public marketplace Planned until security, commercial, operational, support, and governance evidence exists.

### Developers

- Stabilize internal contracts, versioning, testing, examples, change management, and deprecation expectations.
- Define scoped credentials, tenant-safe extension points, audit obligations, abuse controls, and approval requirements.
- Consider a controlled developer preview only after core authentication, authorization, and isolation evidence is sufficient.

### Businesses

- Make core setup, knowledge, customer communication, appointment, messaging, document, follow-up, and decision workflows more reliable where separately approved.
- Reduce repeated administration and make approvals, status, recovery, and audit easier to understand.
- Establish evidence-based onboarding, readiness, and ongoing operating ownership.

### Customers

- Make approved customer journeys coherent, responsive, accessible, private, and business-branded.
- Reduce repeated questions, expose exact status, preserve context, and make human help natural.
- Complete representative authentication, tenant, Storage, messaging, appointment, document, support, accessibility, performance, and security evidence before production certification.

### International expansion

- Build foundations for locale, timezone, language, names, addresses, numbers, consent, translation, right-to-left layout, and accessibility.
- Research legal, privacy, security, data-location, tax, support, commercial, and cultural requirements.
- Launch no market solely from demand signals; market entry remains Planned pending separate readiness and approval.

## Three-year horizon — governed connection

### Platform

- Connect mature platform layers through stable, permission-aware contracts without broad shared access.
- Improve continuity across approved channels while preserving purpose limitation, tenant isolation, and platform-layer separation.
- Support versioned business configuration, policy, lifecycle, recovery, and audit controls with safe defaults.
- Keep status semantics consistent across JOHAI and external-provider boundaries.

### AI

- Expand only AI Employee roles that demonstrate reduced effort, safe boundaries, correct abstention, and measurable business value.
- Introduce reviewable and reversible multi-step assistance within explicit action contracts.
- Improve source quality, conflict handling, policy enforcement, monitoring, and human escalation.
- Do not allow business configuration to override law, safety, privacy, accessibility, or professional limits.

### Marketplace

- Consider a governed marketplace of reviewed integrations, templates, workflows, and specialist services.
- Make provider identity, access, data use, cost, support, compatibility, verification, and conflicts of interest visible.
- Operate continuous monitoring, suspension, incident response, removal, and remedy processes.
- Never substitute popularity or revenue for quality and security evidence.

### Developers

- Consider stable developer interfaces for approved platform capabilities.
- Provide sandboxes, fictional test tenants, scoped credentials, contract tests, audit support, and compatibility guidance.
- Establish review paths for public and private extensions and publish predictable change and incident communication.

### Businesses

- Connect more operating workflows without forcing one business model or one industry vocabulary.
- Improve permission-aware context, approval queues, exception handling, service evidence, and team adoption guidance.
- Let businesses configure brand and customer experience within accessibility, privacy, truth, and security guardrails.

### Customers

- Provide more continuous journeys across authorized business touchpoints while keeping each business relationship distinct.
- Improve customer control over identity, preferences, consent, communication, documents, and human support.
- Expand languages, devices, and access modes only with verified quality and operations.

### International expansion

- Consider a small number of markets only after local legal, security, privacy, accessibility, language, commercial, and support gates pass.
- Build accountable regional support, incident response, content review, and language-quality capacity.
- Document market-specific limitations instead of implying false global uniformity.

## Five-year horizon — extensible contextual platform

### Platform

- Evolve toward a modular platform for coherent business and customer experiences across approved industries.
- Mature identity, consent, policy, event, provenance, context, portability, observability, and recovery infrastructure.
- Support regional deployment or data handling only where lawful, justified, secure, and operationally mature.
- Preserve graceful degradation and reversible change as integrations and modules grow.

### AI

- Support a broader portfolio of independently governed role-specific assistants.
- Improve longitudinal context only within explicit purpose, permission, retention, correction, and deletion boundaries.
- Coordinate approved roles through minimized contracts, never unrestricted shared memory.
- Improve simulation, uncertainty, explanation, and pre-action review while keeping qualified humans responsible for regulated and high-impact decisions.

### Marketplace

- Consider an international ecosystem of verified providers, modules, integrations, and implementation services.
- Provide transparent compatibility, security, accessibility, support, data-use, and quality signals.
- Give businesses controlled installation, permission preview, monitoring, revocation, export, and recovery.
- Establish remedy when a provider fails and prohibit deceptive placement or listings.

### Developers

- Support a mature ecosystem with versioned interfaces, strong tooling, representative testing, review, and fair commercial rules.
- Expose useful event, policy, context, and observability contracts without exposing secrets, internal orchestration, or unrestricted data.
- Maintain a stable core while permitting bounded experimentation and credible migration paths.

### Businesses

- Help businesses coordinate more of the customer lifecycle through approved context and accountable workflows.
- Provide evidence about service quality, effort, follow-through, operating risk, and correction.
- Support industry variation through shared typed modules and configuration, not separate insecure products.
- Keep ownership, portability, retention, deletion, provider choice, and the ability to stop understandable.

### Customers

- Reduce fragmentation across participating businesses without creating an unrestricted cross-business profile.
- Improve customer-controlled consent, preferences, history, documents, communication, portability, and human access.
- Preserve accessible deterministic paths when optional AI is unavailable or unwanted.

### International expansion

- Expand only where governance, language, culture, support, partners, regulation, accessibility, privacy, security, and economics are durable.
- Preserve universal protective standards while implementing transparent local requirements.
- Withdraw, narrow, or delay a market when the North Star cannot be sustained.

## Ten-year horizon — trusted international operating layer

### Platform

- Pursue durable infrastructure for trusted coordination among businesses, customers, developers, and approved providers.
- Support interoperable identity, consent, context, policy, and workflow controls where standards, law, and user choice permit.
- Preserve business autonomy, customer rights, JOHAI accountability, tenant isolation, portability, audit, recovery, and succession at scale.
- Keep the experience calm and useful even as the underlying platform becomes more capable.

### AI

- Pursue highly capable contextual assistance that remains bounded by role, evidence, permission, purpose, and human authority.
- Prepare and coordinate complex work without concealing uncertainty, authorship, or accountability.
- Treat every increase in autonomy as a separate risk decision, never as an inevitable destination.
- Preserve meaningful human takeover and non-AI operation; keep regulated judgment under applicable law and qualified human control.

### Marketplace

- Consider a durable international marketplace governed by transparent participation, verification, permission, accountability, portability, competition, and remedy.
- Support regional and industry specialists without weakening universal trust requirements.
- Make commercial placement, data access, provider responsibility, and conflicts of interest visible.
- Do not tolerate unsafe behavior because an ecosystem has become large or commercially important.

### Developers

- Support developers as long-term platform participants through predictable governance, documentation, tooling, compatibility, and fair change management.
- Enable interoperable innovation that measurably benefits businesses and customers.
- Preserve strict boundaries around identity, context, regulated domains, external action, and tenant data.

### Businesses

- Help businesses deliver prepared, consistent, human-centered service across channels and regions.
- Strengthen business control over identity, knowledge, data, policy, customer relationships, providers, and delegation.
- Make operational intelligence useful without turning employees or customers into surveillance targets.
- Keep JOHAI in the supporting role while the business receives credit for value delivered.

### Customers

- Reduce the effort required to communicate, schedule, exchange documents, obtain support, and understand state across participating businesses.
- Give customers meaningful control over identity, consent, preferences, portability, correction, deletion, and human access.
- Reject compulsory universal profiles, opaque cross-business scoring, manipulative personalization, and hidden data combination.
- Ensure international scale does not reduce dignity, accessibility, privacy, language quality, or local relevance.

### International expansion

- Pursue international presence only where accountable operations, law, privacy, security, accessibility, language quality, customer support, and business support can be maintained.
- Participate responsibly in standards and regulatory dialogue when useful.
- Represent regional differences honestly and decline markets where constitutional operation is not sustainable.

## Roadmap gates

No item advances because it is novel, technically possible, requested by one prospect, or commercially attractive. Before moving from Planned, record:

1. North Star, Constitution, Product Promise, and Company Values alignment.
2. The real problem, baseline, affected people, expected outcome, and current evidence.
3. Owning platform layer, accountable owner, approvers, and operating owner.
4. Identity, tenant, authorization, threat, abuse, and least-privilege evidence.
5. Purpose, minimization, lawful basis, consent, retention, correction, revocation, and deletion.
6. Human authority, professional boundary, escalation, pause, override, and appeal.
7. Responsive, accessibility, language, locale, timezone, and cultural evidence.
8. Failure, provider degradation, monitoring, recovery, rollback, audit, support, and retirement.
9. The mandatory live-schema, migration, read-only verification, human approval, manual execution, and recorded-verification workflow for any database change.
10. Truthful status, customer, sales, investor, and documentation claims.
11. Success, harm, correction, cost, and stop thresholds.
12. Operational, commercial, partner, funding, legal, and support dependencies.

A no, unknown, or unsupported answer narrows, revises, delays, or stops the item.

## Roadmap review and truth

Review may reorder a horizon, split or narrow an initiative, extend research, remove an item, or classify it as deliberately unavailable. Review must preserve decision history and explain why evidence changed the direction.

The roadmap succeeds when it helps JOHAI make disciplined choices, not when every possibility ships. Nothing in this document creates application behavior, database state, a contractual commitment, a market launch, a production capability, or permission to implement.
