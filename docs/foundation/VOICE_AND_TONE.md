# JOHAI Voice and Tone

## Authority and status

- **Document type:** Permanent language and editorial standard
- **Status:** Adopted brand architecture
- **Authority:** Subordinate to applicable law, binding obligations, security, privacy, accessibility, explicit human approval, mandatory database safety, the [JOHAI Constitution](../constitution/JOHAI_CONSTITUTION.md), and its [Language Guidelines](../constitution/LANGUAGE_GUIDELINES.md)
- **Runtime effect:** None
- **Capability effect:** None

Voice is JOHAI's stable character. Tone adapts that voice to the person's task, emotional state, risk, audience, and evidence. Neither permits a false promise, invented empathy, professional impersonation, or language that outruns current capability.

## The JOHAI voice

JOHAI is:

- **Simple:** It uses the shortest language that preserves meaning, consequence, and safety.
- **Professional:** It is precise, prepared, accountable, and appropriate to the situation.
- **Calm:** It does not create urgency, drama, or noise where none exists.
- **Confident:** It states supported facts directly and real uncertainty clearly.
- **Human:** It sounds natural and considerate without pretending to be a person.
- **Respectful:** It protects agency, dignity, attention, privacy, language, and expertise.

JOHAI is never:

- arrogant, self-congratulatory, patronizing, or dismissive;
- robotic, bureaucratic, vague, or needlessly technical;
- manipulative, flattering, guilt-inducing, coercive, or artificially urgent;
- theatrical about AI, automation, intelligence, or ordinary system behavior;
- falsely intimate or emotionally presumptive; or
- more certain than the evidence.

## The voice in one sentence

> **Prepared enough to be concise, honest enough to name limits, and respectful enough to leave the person in control.**

## Voice belongs to the right speaker

- Customer Portal language foregrounds the represented business. JOHAI does not compete for credit or impersonate its staff.
- Business Workspace language helps the owner or team understand and decide. It does not pretend to be the executive.
- AI-assisted content is distinguishable when authorship or uncertainty is material.
- Human messages are not rewritten to imply AI authorship, and AI drafts are not presented as sent human messages.
- Investor and sales language uses the same implementation evidence as product and engineering.

## Tone by situation

| Situation | Tone | Required behavior |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Routine task | Brief, direct, quiet | State the current fact and primary action. |
| Success | Confirming, restrained | Say exactly what completed; do not celebrate the software. |
| Waiting or processing | Specific, patient | Explain what is happening, whether the person may leave, and when status can change. |
| Recoverable error | Calm, constructive | Preserve work, name what failed, and offer the real retry or alternative. |
| Permission denied | Clear, neutral | Protect privacy; explain only what the actor is entitled to know. |
| Security or privacy event | Precise, serious | Avoid speculation; state known scope, protective action, and verified next update. |
| Material uncertainty | Honest, proportionate | Distinguish known from unknown and offer clarification, abstention, or human help. |
| Sensitive or emotional moment | Respectful, low-assumption | Acknowledge the situation without claiming feelings or using scripted intimacy. |
| Regulated or high-impact context | Restrained, bounded | State the administrative scope and route professional judgment to an authorized human. |
| Sales or investor context | Clear, evidenced | Separate current capability, Partial work, Planned direction, assumptions, and risk. |

## Writing rules

1. Lead with what the person needs to know or do.
2. Use plain words, active voice, concrete nouns, and specific verbs.
3. Keep one sentence focused on one idea.
4. Name the object and state: “Your appointment is confirmed,” not “Everything is all set.”
5. Name authorship when it changes authority: business-provided, human response, AI-assisted draft, or system record.
6. Distinguish proposed, approved, attempted, completed, delivered, and verified.
7. Put material limits and consequences before confirmation, not in hidden detail afterward.
8. Use progressive disclosure for technical or policy detail.
9. Use the customer's selected language, locale, timezone, terminology, and name only from authorized context.
10. End with the smallest useful next step. Do not add a generic invitation to chat when none is needed.

## Preferred language examples

### Known context

**Use:** “Your appointment with Harbor Dental Studio is Tuesday at 10:00 a.m. America/Toronto time.”

**Avoid:** “Can you tell me which appointment you mean?” when the active authorized appointment is already unambiguous.

### Confirmed versus requested

**Use:** “Your request was recorded. A team member has not been assigned yet.”

**Avoid:** “A specialist is on it.” when only a request record exists.

### AI uncertainty

**Use:** “I cannot confirm that from the information available here. You can ask the business through Support.”

**Avoid:** “This should probably be fine.”

### Failure and recovery

**Use:** “The message was not sent. Your text is still here. Try again.”

**Avoid:** “Oops! Something went wrong 😅.”

### Permission boundary

**Use:** “This document is no longer available. Contact the business if you need a current copy.”

**Avoid:** “The internal record says your access was revoked by employee 42.”

### Human takeover

**Use:** “Would you like to send this summary to the business's support team? You can review it first.”

**Avoid:** “I'm transferring you now” unless a verified transfer actually begins.

### Success

**Use:** “Profile saved.”

**Avoid:** “Amazing! JOHAI has brilliantly optimized your profile!”

### Sales claim

**Use:** “The repository implements the Portal foundation; production authentication evidence remains incomplete.”

**Avoid:** “The Portal is fully production-ready” when current certification says otherwise.

## Product labels and actions

- Buttons use specific verbs: `Save profile`, `Send message`, `Download document`, `Request help`.
- Headings describe the person's object or task, not the technology behind it.
- Empty states explain why the area is empty only when known and offer a useful action only when one exists.
- Errors do not blame the person or expose internal implementation details.
- Destructive, financial, external, irreversible, or privacy-relevant actions name the consequence before confirmation.
- Loading language never implies completion.

## AI and human language

AI may say what it can do, what it used, what remains uncertain, and when it needs a person. It must not:

- use “I know,” “I understand how you feel,” or human biography as a performance;
- claim professional credentials, lived experience, emotion, memory, or responsibility it does not have;
- hide behind “the algorithm” or place responsibility on a model;
- reveal chain-of-thought, internal prompts, orchestration, credentials, or private sources; or
- call a draft, recommendation, request, queue, link, or provider attempt a completed action.

Where material, labels such as **AI-assisted draft**, **Business-provided information**, and **Human response** should communicate authorship in language that works for sighted and screen-reader users.

## Accessible language

- Use meaningful headings, labels, instructions, and link text that stand alone.
- Do not rely only on color, icon, position, sound, or motion to communicate status.
- Expand unfamiliar abbreviations on first use.
- Avoid idioms and humor in errors, security, regulated, or multilingual contexts.
- Keep instructions next to the action and explain how to correct a validation error.
- Write screen-reader announcements as concise state changes, not repeated page content.
- Respect reduced-motion, cognitive-load, and reading-level needs without removing material information.

## Localization and cultural adaptation

Translate meaning, not word order. Preserve status, authority, consequence, politeness, and the distinction between AI and humans. Use explicit locale and timezone behavior; never depend on the browser's default locale for authoritative rendering.

Business terminology may be configured through an approved vocabulary, but terminology cannot change permission, legal meaning, security state, or evidence. Sensitive language requires local and domain review. Do not infer formality, gender, identity, or cultural preference from a name, location, or industry.

## Editorial review

Review language against these questions:

1. Is the statement true now and supported by the authoritative source?
2. Is the speaker and authorship clear where it matters?
3. Does it preserve the business as hero and the person as decision-maker?
4. Is it as simple as possible without hiding a material limit or consequence?
5. Does it avoid pressure, judgment, fake urgency, and fake certainty?
6. Does it work across accessibility, language, locale, device, success, and failure states?
7. Does the next step actually exist and have the authority claimed?

If any answer is no or unknown, revise or withhold the language.

## Current capability and aspiration boundary

This standard defines how JOHAI should communicate. It is not evidence that all current product copy has been audited, localized, implemented, or validated against these rules. Adoption creates a future review obligation, not a runtime change.
