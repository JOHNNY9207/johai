- **Foundation Status:** COMPLETE

The JOHAI Foundation is complete as a governance and product-direction system. Version 1.0 is the official baseline for future architecture, product, design, AI, language, brand, and implementation decisions.

“Complete” means the Foundation documents have been adopted, reconciled, indexed, and placed under change control. It does not mean that every product capability is implemented, every interface conforms, every AI Employee exists, or any product layer is production-certified.

## Lock rule

No Foundation document may be changed casually, silently, or as incidental feature work. Every future change requires:

1. an explicit user request to reopen Foundation work;
2. a documented architectural decision that classifies and justifies the change;
3. review against the Constitution and affected protective requirements;
4. explicit approval from the designated human authority recorded by name or role in the decision;
5. version and release-record updates proportionate to the change; and
6. link, consistency, status-truth, and documentation validation.

The full process is defined in [Foundation Change Policy](FOUNDATION_CHANGE_POLICY.md). An AI system may prepare analysis or a proposal but may not initiate an unrequested strategic-documentation epic, approve a Foundation change, or silently reinterpret a locked rule.

## Authority order

The lock does not alter the established authority hierarchy:

1. applicable law, binding contracts, security, privacy, accessibility, explicit human approval, and mandatory database safety;
2. the JOHAI Constitution;
3. constitutional companion principles;
4. the locked strategic, contextual-intelligence, brand, culture, and roadmap Foundation;
5. approved architecture, policies, manuals, specifications, and implementation records.

Lower-level work may be more specific or more protective. It may not weaken or contradict the locked Foundation.

## Ready-for-development status

The Foundation is ready to govern development. This declaration authorizes no feature by itself. Each implementation still requires scoped product approval, correct platform-layer ownership, constitutional review, security, privacy, accessibility, testing, operations, truthful capability status, and the mandatory database workflow when applicable.

The next development priorities are:

6. Business Brain V2.

No further strategic-documentation epic should be proposed unless the user explicitly requests it.
