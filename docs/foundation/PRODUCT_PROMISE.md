# The JOHAI Product Promise

## Authority and status

- **Document type:** Permanent product promise
- **Status:** Adopted strategic standard
- **Authority:** Subordinate to applicable law, binding obligations, security, privacy, accessibility, explicit human approval, mandatory database safety, and the [JOHAI Constitution](../constitution/JOHAI_CONSTITUTION.md)
- **Runtime effect:** None
- **Capability effect:** None; each product claim still requires current implementation and operational evidence

The Product Promise defines what every JOHAI experience should earn for customers and businesses. It is an acceptance standard, not advertising copy, a warranty of unavailable behavior, or evidence that a Planned capability exists.

> **JOHAI makes the next responsible step easier to understand and complete, using only authorized context and never trading trust or human authority for apparent intelligence.**

## How the promises work together

The eight promises are cumulative. Speed without reliability is rework. Context without permission is surveillance. Transparency without clarity is noise. Consistency without respect is rigid. Business value without customer value is extraction.

No promise authorizes JOHAI to weaken another promise, the Constitution, accessibility, privacy, security, tenant isolation, or human approval. When promises appear to conflict, JOHAI protects people, truth, and authoritative state; narrows scope; and explains the real limitation.

## 1. Speed

### Promise

JOHAI should reduce the total time required to understand and complete a legitimate task.

### What it means

- Use current authorized context before asking someone to repeat information.
- Make the relevant state and primary action easy to find.
- Remove unnecessary navigation, typing, waiting, duplication, and coordination.
- Include correction, recovery, and human handoff in the time calculation.

### What it never means

- skipping consent, accessibility, authorization, review, or a consequential confirmation;
- claiming completion before an authoritative system confirms it; or
- moving fast locally while creating downstream errors or support work.

### Evidence

Measure end-to-end completion time, repetition, avoidable steps, correction, recovery, abandonment, and time to appropriate human help against a defined baseline.

## 2. Trust

### Promise

JOHAI should behave in a way that deserves confidence through every success, limitation, and failure.

### What it means

- Protect identity, tenant, role, customer, source, and platform-layer boundaries.
- Distinguish facts, inferences, recommendations, drafts, approvals, attempts, and verified outcomes.
- Admit what is unknown, unavailable, stale, or unverified before it causes harm.
- Preserve meaningful human control, correction, revocation, and appeal.

### What it never means

- performing certainty, hiding AI authorship, or using polished language to conceal risk;
- asking customers to trust a claim unsupported by evidence; or
- using one success to excuse an unresolved security or privacy blocker.

### Evidence

Measure boundary violations, unsupported claims, fabricated actions, correction rates, revocation behavior, incident recovery, trust feedback, and truthful status across representative conditions.

## 3. Context

### Promise

JOHAI should prepare the task with the minimum authorized, relevant, current context so people do not have to start over.

### What it means

- Resolve actor, tenant, role, purpose, source visibility, provenance, and freshness before use.
- Carry safe context through a workflow and make consequential values reviewable.
- Ask only the smallest question needed when material ambiguity remains.
- Forget, suppress, or stop using context when purpose, permission, freshness, or relevance ends.

### What it never means

- inferring permission from availability, industry, history, or model confidence;
- exposing CRM internals, private notes, raw internal knowledge, prompts, secrets, or another tenant; or
- surprising a person with sensitive or unrelated information.

### Evidence

Measure repeated questions avoided, context accuracy, provenance and freshness, correct suppression, revocation, tenant switching, and customer perception of relevance versus intrusion.

## 4. Transparency

### Promise

JOHAI should make material state, authorship, consequence, uncertainty, and responsibility understandable at the moment they matter.

### What it means

- Say whether content comes from an authoritative record, the business, a human, or AI assistance.
- State what will happen before a consequential action and what actually happened afterward.
- Explain material limits in plain language and expose deeper detail progressively.
- Represent human notification, assignment, response, and external-provider state only from evidence.

### What it never means

- exposing internal prompts, orchestration, security controls, or private sources;
- overwhelming people with implementation detail; or
- using a disclaimer to excuse an unsafe or misleading design.

### Evidence

Test whether representative users can identify source, status, consequence, AI versus human authorship, unresolved uncertainty, and the real next step.

## 5. Respect

### Promise

JOHAI should respect people's attention, time, dignity, agency, privacy, language, accessibility, expertise, and relationship with the represented business.

### What it means

- Keep interruption rare, useful, dismissible, and proportionate.
- Offer meaningful choice, correction, cancellation, and human help.
- Use calm language without pressure, judgment, flattery, or artificial intimacy.
- Treat accessibility, localization, and inclusive participation as part of correctness.

### What it never means

- manipulation, manufactured urgency, dark patterns, engagement traps, or surveillance;
- making a customer feel scored, controlled, or managed; or
- treating disabled people, languages, devices, or cultures as edge cases.

### Evidence

Measure unwanted interruption, dismissal, correction, accessibility completion, language and device parity, consent quality, complaints, and the ability to decline without penalty.

## 6. Reliability

### Promise

JOHAI should preserve authoritative state and provide a safe, understandable path through delay, failure, and recovery.

### What it means

- Design loading, empty, denied, expired, degraded, retry, duplicate, provider-failure, and rollback behavior.
- Prevent duplicate or unauthorized actions.
- Keep optional AI from becoming a dependency for essential deterministic work.
- Preserve the last valid state when a proposed or generated step fails.

### What it never means

- optimistic status that masks uncertainty;
- silent fallback to a broader tenant, identity, source, or permission; or
- a happy-path demonstration presented as operational readiness.

### Evidence

Measure availability appropriate to the service, error containment, retry safety, duplicate prevention, recovery completion, monitoring, incident response, and restoration of authoritative state.

## 7. Consistency

### Promise

JOHAI should apply the same truth, permission, interaction, language, and safety rules across routes, devices, industries, channels, and AI roles.

### What it means

- Build shared contracts, semantic patterns, tokens, terminology, and status models.
- Adapt by governed configuration and typed modules rather than scattered industry exceptions.
- Preserve meaning across locale, timezone, responsive layout, and provider state.
- Make a familiar action behave predictably without erasing legitimate domain differences.

### What it never means

- forcing one workflow onto every industry or person;
- duplicating portals, navigation, or authorization rules; or
- making unsafe behavior consistent instead of correcting it.

### Evidence

Test the same contract across representative surfaces, tenants, roles, industries, languages, viewports, assistive technologies, providers, and failure states.

## 8. Business value

### Promise

JOHAI should create durable operating value for the business while improving the legitimate customer outcome.

### What it means

- Reduce manual reconstruction, duplicate work, avoidable delay, and preventable correction.
- Improve the owner's understanding of what changed, what matters, and what needs judgment.
- Make bounded delegation more reviewable and accountable.
- Strengthen the business's service, knowledge, resilience, and customer relationships.

### What it never means

- maximizing AI activity, messages, dashboards, engagement, or automation as ends;
- extracting value through confusion, pressure, lock-in, or hidden data use; or
- claiming savings, growth, conversion, or return without representative evidence.

### Evidence

Measure time and work removed, task completion, error and support reduction, service capacity, customer outcome, decision quality, operating resilience, and sustainable economic value. Product usage alone is not proof.

## Promise review

Every material proposal records:

1. which promises it advances and the baseline evidence;
2. which promises it could weaken and the protective controls;
3. the customer and business outcome;
4. how failure and recovery preserve the promises;
5. the evidence required before release and before making a public claim; and
6. whether the Constitution and [Decision Framework](../constitution/DECISION_FRAMEWORK.md) pass.

A promise with no measurable acceptance evidence remains intent. A release that violates one promise cannot compensate with a high average across the others.

## Current capability and aspiration boundary

This Product Promise is an adopted standard for future decisions. It does not state that every current JOHAI route, integration, AI Employee, industry workflow, or production environment already satisfies every promise.

Current implementation and production status remain governed by the [Product Fact Sheet](../PRODUCT_FACT_SHEET.md), sprint evidence, and operating records. Gaps must be labeled `Partial`, `Planned`, or deliberately unavailable until resolved and verified.
