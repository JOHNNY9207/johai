## Architecture status

**Adopted and frozen.** The Constitution remains the highest internal product-design authority. Constitutional principles, strategic identity, Contextual Intelligence, brand, voice, culture, AI Employee rules, and roadmap direction are reconciled beneath it.

Foundation V1 defines governance constraints and direction. It does not implement a context engine, AI Employee runtime, marketplace, Super Admin, Business Brain V2, Customer Portal certification, or Business Workspace certification.

## Documentation status

**Complete for Foundation Version 1.0.** The canonical documents are present, indexed, linked from the repository and documentation entry points, integrated into audience manuals, and recorded in project history and the Product Fact Sheet.

Future modifications are controlled by `docs/foundation/FOUNDATION_CHANGE_POLICY.md`. No Foundation document is a draft after this release.

## Governance status

**Locked.** Every future implementation must comply with the Constitution, North Star, Manifesto, Product Promise, Company Values, AI Principles, UX Principles, Design Principles, Contextual Intelligence, and Brand System.

No implementation may violate or silently reinterpret these documents. A Foundation change requires an explicit user request, architectural decision, named human approval, version update, and release validation. AI cannot approve a change.

## Testing status

Documentation validation for Foundation V1 includes:

- required-file and required-section coverage;
- authority-hierarchy and status-truth review;
- local Markdown-link resolution;
- duplicate-section, formatting, encoding, and trailing-whitespace review;
- independent read-only governance review; and
- confirmation that no application, UI, API, authentication, SQL, migration, or runtime change is part of this release.

No runtime test suite was required or executed for the Foundation lock because runtime files did not change. Product and certification testing remains separate and must be completed by each implementation sprint.

## Known limitations

- Foundation completion is not product completion or production readiness.
- Existing application surfaces have not all been certified for complete Foundation conformity.
- Brand architecture does not constitute a finished visual asset, token, component, or theme implementation.
- AI Employee role principles do not establish that a named role is implemented, deployed, autonomous, or professionally authorized.
- Contextual Intelligence remains a design doctrine; a unified runtime and its measurements remain separate implementation work.
- The Long-Term Roadmap is directional and is not a delivery, funding, market-entry, or commercial commitment.
- Customer Portal and Business Workspace production certifications retain their own evidence and blockers.

## Ready-for-development declaration

**Ready for development: YES, for separately approved and scoped implementation under Foundation V1 governance.**

This declaration means future development has a stable governing baseline. It does not authorize a specific feature, database change, release, or production claim. Each initiative must still pass its platform, Constitution, security, privacy, accessibility, operational, validation, documentation, and human-approval gates.
