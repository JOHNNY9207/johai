# Using the AI Chat

The chat answers questions from configured context, collects lead information, and can direct visitors to booking. Review conversations and keep business knowledge current. Human approval remains necessary for unsupported or high-impact commitments.

## Status discipline

See [Product Fact Sheet](../PRODUCT_FACT_SHEET.md) for Implemented, Partial, and Planned classifications. Update this chapter when the corresponding product behavior changes.

## What contextual assistance should feel like

The [JOHAI Constitution](../constitution/JOHAI_CONSTITUTION.md) requires AI to be useful, calm, honest, permission-aware, and subordinate to the represented business. JOHAI's permanent customer-experience rule is: **The AI never asks for information it can already infer from context.** When enough authorized information is available, a future contextual interaction should offer the next useful explanation or action directly. When context is incomplete, JOHAI should ask only for the missing detail needed to continue.

Assistance should be calm, concise, optional, and easy to dismiss. JOHAI should not interrupt a completed or critical task, pretend to be a person, pressure a decision, or claim that a suggested action happened. Material uncertainty should be explained, and medical, legal, financial, safety, emergency, privacy, or policy-limited questions should move to an appropriate human channel.

The current website chat remains the implemented interface described above. Smart documents, contextual appointment suggestions, proactive support, translation, human takeover operations, and other [Smart Interaction Patterns](../philosophy/SMART_INTERACTION_PATTERNS.md) are **Planned** unless another product record explicitly marks them Implemented.

Customer-facing assistance may use only information the business has approved for that customer and workflow. It cannot expose internal CRM data, Business Brain internals, private notes, prompts, billing internals, raw Knowledge Center files, system orchestration, or another customer's information.

The full standards for authorship, uncertainty, pressure, consent, silence, and human takeover are in [AI Principles](../constitution/AI_PRINCIPLES.md) and [Language Guidelines](../constitution/LANGUAGE_GUIDELINES.md).
